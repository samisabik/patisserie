# Lemon Tart

*Makes: one 9" / 23 cm tart · Active time: 45 min · Total time: ~3 hrs (incl. chilling)*

> Classic tarte au citron — crisp pâte sucrée with a smooth, sharp lemon filling.
> Example recipe to show the format; edit or delete it.

## Ingredients

**Pâte sucrée**
- 125 g plain flour
- 50 g icing sugar
- 75 g cold unsalted butter, cubed
- 1 egg yolk
- 1 tbsp cold water
- pinch of salt

**Lemon filling**
- 3 eggs
- 150 g caster sugar
- 120 ml lemon juice (about 3 lemons)
- zest of 1 lemon
- 100 ml double cream

## Method

1. Rub butter into the flour, icing sugar, and salt until it looks like coarse sand.
2. Mix in the yolk and water until it just comes together. Flatten into a disc, wrap, chill 30 min.
3. Roll out and line the tin. Chill another 20 min, then blind-bake at 180 °C for 15 min; remove weights and bake 5–8 min more until pale gold.
4. Whisk the eggs, sugar, lemon juice, and zest, then stir in the cream.
5. Pour into the warm shell and bake at 150 °C for 25–30 min, until just set with a slight wobble.
6. Cool completely, then chill before slicing. Dust with icing sugar to serve.

## Notes

- Keeps 2–3 days covered in the fridge.
- For a smoother top, strain the filling before pouring.
- Pâte sucrée freezes well — make a double batch and freeze half.
