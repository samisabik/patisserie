# Galetous

## Ingredients

- 400 g buckwheat flour
- 100 g plain flour
- 10 g fresh baker's yeast
- 1 L water
- 15 g salt

## Recette

1. The day before, make a starter with the yeast, 100 g of the buckwheat flour, 100 g of the plain flour and a little water.
2. The next day, add the remaining flour and water along with the salt. Mix well.
3. To judge the right thickness of the batter: take a small spoon, dip it into the batter and turn it over rounded side up; it should be coated with a 1 mm layer (add water if necessary).
4. Leave to rise for 3 hours in a warm place. Cook in a frying pan greased with oil, like crêpes.

## Notes

In the Limousin region, galetous traditionally accompanied dishes in sauce such as civet or stew. Nowadays they are usually served as an aperitif, spread with grilhons (Limousin rillettes), rolled up and cut into sections, ideally presented in a hollowed-out rye bread loaf.
